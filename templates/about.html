About the Project

This project focuses on analyzing simultaneous EEG and fMRI recordings to predict brain states during sleep. Using advanced machine learning techniques, we've developed a fusion model that integrates both EEG features (such as Delta, Theta, Alpha, Beta, and Gamma power bands) and fMRI features (BOLD signal means and variances) to accurately classify different states of consciousness.

Our Approach

We've implemented a multimodal neural network architecture that processes EEG and fMRI data streams separately before fusing them at deeper layers. This approach leverages the complementary nature of these two neuroimaging modalities:

  • EEG: High temporal resolution that captures rapid oscillatory neural activity
  • fMRI: High spatial resolution that reveals detailed brain activation patterns

Key Features

  • Integration of EEG (electrophysiological) and fMRI (hemodynamic) neural signals
  • Classification of multiple brain states (Rest, Eyes Open, Eyes Closed)
  • Comprehensive visualizations of feature distributions and correlations
  • Analysis of prediction confidence through probability distributions
  • Confusion matrix visualization for labeled data to assess model performance

Research Applications

This technology has potential applications in:

  • Sleep medicine and sleep disorder diagnosis
  • Consciousness research
  • Brain-computer interfaces
  • Neurofeedback systems
  • Monitoring of anesthesia depth

Technology Stack

  • Data Processing: NumPy, Pandas
  • Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
  • Machine Learning: Custom neural network architecture
